Introduction

Traditional approaches to Machine Learning models in financial markets are generally borne from a qauntativistic approach to the market. This means they attempt to find corrrelations between market indicators such as inflation, interest rates, and other stocks, and they're common for good reason. Modern trading algorithms have been able to find great success with this approach and today huge number of trades are executed by these algorithms.

However, this clasic approach does have blind spots. The most notable example of this is the recent surge in gamestop's stock price during 2021. This surge was driven by a group of retail investors collaborating on messaging boards like reddit and twitter to buy up shares of a stock that was heavily shorted, leading to collosal losses for the hedge funds that relied on those same qauntativistic algorithms which predicted the stock bounce back. However, due to the collective action of the retail investors, the stock price continued to rise, further driving corporate losses far past the point where they could divest.

The focus of this project is to ask whether we can adapt our clasical models to the new era of social media and widespread communication to better predict humanistic trends in the market. While the scope of this project was limited to a specific stock (NVDA), this approach holds promise for a wide range of applications such as predicting ressessions or market crashes that can have widespread effects on the economy, and expecially on the most vulnerable members of society.

In doing this project we wanted understand and tackle the real life problems that face data data scientists. In this pursuit, we identified three main components: Data Collection, Data Processing/Analysis, and Communication of Results
